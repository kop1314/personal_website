Backstory

This Android Application is a real-time chat app which I built back in 2017 when I took the course, CS126 Software Design Studio, in the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). This project was built with the use of Firebase, a real-time database developed by Google. My goal was designing and implementing an application that building up the network where alumni, faculty or current college students can share their employment resources, such as job posts and job referrals with students in UIUC. Unfortunately, it was hard to accomplish my goal by working on this project in less than one month by myself. But my Android Application is enough for users to chat with friends!

Registration

In order to access the platform, you have to first create a user account. For the email address, a fake email is good enough because there is no confirmation email. But the fake email must be in valid email format, a @ symbol with an email prefix on its left and an email domain on its right (email_prefix@email_domain). An example of a fake email will be example@mail.com where example is the email prefix and mail.com is the email domain. Simply filling out the below form and click REGISTER will send you to the chat page.

Alternately, you can login to the chat page with the below email and password:
email: newuser@gmail.com
password: newuser

Find your friend

Now, it is time to look for your friends and add them to your chat list. To find your friend, click on toggle on the top right corner first. Then, press Add Friends and look for your friends by typing their names in the search bar.

Start chatting

After you added your friends to your chat list, you can now find your friend in your chat page.

To start a chat, click on a name of anyone of your friends you would like to talk to. Next, a chatroom will be created for the conversation between you and your friend.
